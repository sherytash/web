Research

My research sits at the intersection of strategic management, applied AI, and formal modeling. I study how organizations make consequential decisions under technological change — from regulation and lobbying to firm strategy and labor markets.

A recurring thread is using large language models as a research instrument: to simulate human decision-making, forecast the results of behavioral experiments, and probe how well AI can stand in for real people. I trained as a mathematician before moving into management, and I’m drawn to problems where careful formal reasoning meets messy organizational reality.

  • How faithfully can large language models simulate human decision-making?
  • How do firms and institutions respond when technology reshapes the rules?
